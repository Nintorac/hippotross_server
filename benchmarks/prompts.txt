# RWKV Benchmark Prompts
# Various sizes and complexities for comprehensive performance testing
# Lines starting with # are comments

# === SHORT PROMPTS (Simple Q&A) ===
What is 2+2?
What is the capital of France?
Name three primary colors.
What year did World War II end?
Who wrote Romeo and Juliet?

# === MEDIUM PROMPTS (Explanations) ===
Explain how photosynthesis works in plants.
What are the main differences between Python and JavaScript?
Describe the water cycle in simple terms.
How does a combustion engine work?
What causes earthquakes and how are they measured?

# === LONGER PROMPTS (Detailed explanations) ===
Explain the theory of relativity, covering both special and general relativity, and give examples of how they affect our daily lives.
Describe the process of machine learning, including supervised and unsupervised learning, with examples of each approach.
What is quantum computing? Explain how it differs from classical computing and discuss potential applications.
Explain the human immune system, including how it identifies and fights pathogens, and how vaccines work.
Describe the history and evolution of the internet, from ARPANET to modern cloud computing.

# === CREATIVE TASKS ===
Write a haiku about programming.
Write a short poem about the ocean at sunset.
Create a brief story about a robot learning to paint.
Write a limerick about a programmer debugging code.
Compose a short fairy tale about a dragon who was afraid of fire.

# === ANALYTICAL TASKS ===
Compare and contrast renewable and non-renewable energy sources, discussing advantages, disadvantages, and future outlook for each.
Analyze the pros and cons of remote work versus office work, considering productivity, work-life balance, and company culture.
Discuss the ethical implications of artificial intelligence in healthcare, including privacy concerns, decision-making authority, and potential biases.
Evaluate the impact of social media on modern society, covering mental health, political discourse, and information dissemination.
Examine the causes and potential solutions for climate change, including individual actions, policy changes, and technological innovations.
